Warm, Comforting Kimchi Jjigae

As the cooler seasons approach, I can’t help but crave a steaming bowl of Korea’s greatest dish (in my very humble opinion), Kimchi Jjigae. It’s one of my favorite go-to dishes when I need a healthy and flavorful staple for the week that reheats perfectly everytime.

I hope you’ll try, and more importantly, enjoy my recipe for kimchi jjigae!

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 5 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 cup pre-made kimchi (or make your own, if you so choose)
  • 1/2 cup green onion
  • 1/2 pack of frozen rolled beef (I buy mine at either Costco or HMart!)
  • 1 tube silken tofu
  • 1-2 tbsp gochujang
  • 4 cups chicken broth/stock or 4 cups of water with 4 chicken bullion cubes
  • Sesame oil

How to Make Your Kimchi-Jjigae:

  1. Heat a neutral oil in a pot over medium heat. I usually go for vegetable oil.
  2. Once hot, add in the whites of your green onion and garlic, reserve the green part for later. Sautee for about 1 minute.
  3. Add in the kimchi and gochujang and sautee for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add in your 4 cups of broth (I prefer 4 cups of water mixed with 4 chicken buillon cubes for a richer flavor!) and bring to a boil.
  5. Once boiled, reduce to a simmer and let cook for another 15 minutes.
  6. Add in frozen rolled beef and allow to cook in the broth, then add in your silken tofu.
  7. Top with sesame oil and green onion, and enjoy!
